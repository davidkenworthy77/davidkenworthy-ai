Great question. The honest answer is: fewer than you'd think.

My daily drivers are **Claude** (for pretty much everything: writing, coding, research, brainstorming) and **Claude Code** (for building and managing this site and other projects).

I use **GPT-4o** occasionally when I want a second opinion or for specific tasks where it performs differently. And **Copilot** is there in the background in VS Code, though I find myself using it less as Claude Code handles more of my workflow.

That's basically it for AI-specific tools. The real answer to "what tools do you use" is on my [Stack page](/stack). Most of my productivity comes from non-AI tools like Linear, Raycast, and good old-fashioned Markdown files.

The meta-lesson: don't collect AI tools. Find one or two that fit your workflow and go deep.
