I don't learn it fast. I just learn it in public, so it looks faster than it is.

My process:
1. **Pick one thing.** Not three frameworks. Not a whole new stack. One thing.
2. **Build something small with it.** Not a tutorial. A real (tiny) thing that solves a real (tiny) problem.
3. **Write about what I learned.** This is the secret weapon. Writing forces you to actually understand it.
4. **Ship it.** Imperfect, incomplete, whatever. Ship it.

The Lab section of this site exists specifically for this. Every experiment is me learning something in public.

The other trick: I use AI to accelerate the boring parts. Claude is incredible for getting past the "how do I set up the project" phase and into the "how does this actually work" phase. The setup used to take hours. Now it takes minutes.
